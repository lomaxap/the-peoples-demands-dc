Read the Demands

The following are the People’s Demands of Washington, DC for the COVID-19 pandemic. This list represents the thoughts and contributions of thousands of DC residents and organizations representing the people.

These demands are imperative for our survival, our growth and are a reflection of the DC we want to live in.

Read More

We acknowledge that this list may be limited and that it would not be possible to include everyone’s specific demands. We consolidated and prioritized the demands that were reflected by the most people, always prioritizing those experiencing the most harm.

We also acknowledge that while there was not as much representation from marginalized voices as we were hoping with the survey respondents, the responses do show that the recommendations are supported by a cross-section of the District’s racial and income groups. The demands below are the results of an initial, quick turnaround survey. We will continue to engage and grow the number of people who engage with the survey and with our organizing process around the demands.

We recognize that many of these demands take full plans, budgets and details. Many organizations have monetary breakdowns of the specific demands and can be contacted for specific plans.

About

We are a group of Black and brown people deeply invested in building the power and unity of poor and working class people in DC.

We came together through the DC Mutual Aid Network, and are committed to co-creating demands with the people of DC—our neighbors, homies, lovers, teachers, co-workers and more.

We are people who live in DC, work in DC, raise children in DC, go to church in DC, graduated college in DC, went to high school in DC, teach in DC, love in DC, organize in DC, and people who have called DC "home" for multiple generations.

We speak multiple languages, some of us work and some of us are out of work and some of us work and get paid a wage too low to really call it living.

But most importantly, we are people who believe the government can and must do more to protect and provide for the people of this city.
