As the COVID-19 crisis continues to develop, it is critical that we develop a set of demands that protect our people and primarily reflect the beliefs and needs of folks that are Black, brown, immigrants, indigenous, low-income and native Washingtonians.

We are developing a list of demands with the help of community members, students of all ages, organizers, advocates and activists. We want to make sure that our finalized list of demands has been shared with and supported by as many DC folks as possible, particularly Black, brown, immigrant, low-income, indigenous and native Washingtonian residents. About

We are a group of Black and brown people deeply invested in building the power and unity of poor and working class people in DC.

We came together through the DC Mutual Aid Network, and are committed to co-creating demands with the people of DC—our neighbors, homies, lovers, teachers, co-workers and more.

We are people who live in DC, work in DC, raise children in DC, go to church in DC, graduated college in DC, went to high school in DC, teach in DC, love in DC, organize in DC, and people who have called DC "home" for multiple generations.

We speak multiple languages, some of us work and some of us are out of work and some of us work and get paid a wage too low to really call it living.

But most importantly, we are people who believe the government can and must do more to protect and provide for the people of this city.
